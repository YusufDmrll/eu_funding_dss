# Demo Project Inputs

These examples are suitable for a feedback session. They include a clear challenge, solution, users, European relevance, maturity, and expected impact.

## 1. Critical raw materials recycling

We are developing a pilot process to recover rare earth elements and other critical raw materials from industrial and electronic waste. The technology combines selective separation, material traceability, and recovered-material quality validation with European recycling operators and manufacturers. The project will demonstrate the process at pilot scale, increase the supply of high-quality secondary raw materials, and reduce European dependence on imported strategic materials.

## 2. Port shore power and green harbour

We are developing an energy-management solution for European ports that coordinates shore power, renewable electricity, battery storage, and berth demand. Port authorities and terminal operators will use the system to reduce vessel emissions at berth, manage electricity peaks, and plan cost-effective infrastructure upgrades. The solution is ready for validation in operational port environments and will support cleaner, more resilient harbour energy systems.

## 3. Hydrogen infrastructure

The project will demonstrate green-hydrogen production, storage, and distribution infrastructure in an industrial port cluster. It targets port authorities, terminal operators, shipping companies, heavy-transport providers, and nearby industrial users. The pilot will integrate renewable electricity with hydrogen logistics, validate safe operational use, and reduce emissions from industrial energy demand and hard-to-electrify transport.

## 4. Maritime logistics optimisation

We are building a digital optimisation platform for ports, vessel operators, freight terminals, and logistics providers. The platform will combine vessel schedules, cargo-flow data, terminal capacity, and multimodal connections to reduce waiting times and improve supply-chain resilience. It will be piloted in two European port ecosystems and aims to reduce fuel consumption, congestion, and freight-transport emissions.

## 5. Critical infrastructure security

We are developing a security and resilience platform for critical port infrastructure using sensor fusion, anomaly detection, and real-time operational monitoring. Port authorities and terminal operators will use it to detect physical and cyber threats, protect essential logistics operations, and coordinate crisis response. The project will validate the technology in a live port setting and improve preparedness against disruption of European transport and supply-chain infrastructure.

## Weak example 1

> We want to build an innovative AI platform that uses data to improve services across Europe.

Why it needs more detail:

- No sector or operational challenge is defined.
- Target users and data sources are unclear.
- The technology, maturity, and intended impact are too broad.
- Many unrelated AI calls could appear superficially relevant.

Better input should name the industry, user, decision being improved, pilot setting, and measurable outcome.

## Weak example 2

> Smart green innovation for Europe.

Why it needs more detail:

- It does not describe a project or solution.
- There is no target user, geography, maturity, or implementation context.
- “Green” and “innovation” are too generic for defensible matching.

Better input should describe the environmental problem, proposed solution, beneficiaries, demonstration activity, and expected European impact.
